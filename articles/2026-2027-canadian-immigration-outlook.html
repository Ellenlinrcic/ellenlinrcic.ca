2026–2027 加拿大移民政策展望：哪些机会正在打开？哪些窗口正在收紧？

2026年，加拿大移民政策进入新一轮调整期。IRCC已经明确表示，将对 Express Entry 进行近年来最大规模的改革，包括整合经济类移民项目、调整 CRS 评分体系，以及重新设计类别邀请（Category Selection）。

虽然许多细节尚未正式公布，但结合近几个月的抽签情况和政府已经释放出的政策方向，未来两年的移民趋势已经越来越清晰。

一、加拿大将更加重视真正紧缺的人才

过去，高学历、高语言成绩往往意味着更高的 CRS 分数。但未来，加拿大可能更加关注申请人的职业是否符合劳动力市场需求。

近几个月持续进行的医疗、教育、法语以及高级管理人员类别抽签，都反映出 IRCC 正在通过定向邀请，引进特定行业的人才，而不仅仅依赖综合分数排名。

二、加拿大工作经验仍然具有优势

虽然 Express Entry 未来可能整合现有三个经济类项目，但这并不意味着加拿大工作经验的重要性下降。

无论是 CEC 抽签，还是近期新增的高级管理人员类别，都显示政府仍然优先考虑已经在加拿大工作、能够较快融入劳动力市场的申请人。

三、高薪职业或将迎来新的机会

IRCC 已经透露，未来 CRS 评分可能加入高薪职业（High-wage Occupations）相关因素。

如果改革正式实施，一些薪资水平较高、对加拿大经济贡献较大的职业，未来可能获得额外优势。这也意味着，申请人的职业和收入水平，可能比过去更加重要。

四、部分传统加分项目可能调整

与此同时，一些现有 CRS 加分项目也可能发生变化，例如加拿大短期学历、法语额外加分以及兄弟姐妹加分等。

目前这些内容仍属于改革方向，最终是否实施以及具体实施时间，仍需等待 IRCC 正式公布。

哪些申请人更值得关注未来变化？

综合目前释放出的政策信号，未来较具优势的申请人可能包括：

  • 已拥有加拿大工作经验的申请人；
  • 医疗、教育、技工等紧缺行业专业人士；
  • 高级管理人员及其他高薪职业从业者；
  • 符合未来 Category Selection 重点行业的人才。

相反，对于主要依赖部分 CRS 额外加分项目的申请人，则建议密切关注政策变化，尽早规划申请，以减少未来改革可能带来的影响。

结语

加拿大并没有减少对经济移民的需求，而是在重新定义需要什么样的人才。

未来，单纯依赖高学历或高语言成绩可能已不足以保持竞争力。职业背景、加拿大工作经验以及是否符合重点类别，预计将在未来的移民体系中扮演越来越重要的角色。

对于计划移民加拿大的人来说，及早了解政策方向，并根据自身背景调整规划，将更有机会把握未来几年的移民窗口。

以上分析基于 IRCC 目前已公布的政策方向、公开信息及近期抽签趋势。具体改革内容及实施时间仍以加拿大移民部未来正式公布的政策为准。

2026–2027 Canadian Immigration Outlook: Which Opportunities Are Emerging and Which Doors May Be Closing?

Canada’s immigration system is entering another period of significant change. Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) has announced plans to undertake one of the most substantial reforms to Express Entry in recent years, including restructuring economic immigration programs, revising the Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS), and refining category-based selection.

Although many details have yet to be finalized, recent invitation rounds and policy announcements already provide a clearer picture of where Canada’s immigration system is heading over the next two years.

1. Greater Focus on Labour Market Needs

In the past, applicants with strong education, high language scores, and extensive work experience often had the best chance of receiving an Invitation to Apply (ITA).

Going forward, Canada is expected to place greater emphasis on occupations that address genuine labour shortages.

Recent category-based draws targeting healthcare professionals, educators, French-speaking candidates, and senior managers demonstrate that IRCC is increasingly selecting applicants based on labour market demand rather than CRS scores alone.

2. Canadian Work Experience Remains a Key Advantage

Although Express Entry programs may eventually be consolidated, Canadian work experience is likely to remain highly valuable.

Recent Canadian Experience Class (CEC) draws and the introduction of the Senior Management category suggest that applicants who have already integrated into Canada’s labour market will continue to enjoy a competitive advantage.

3. High-Wage Occupations May Receive Additional Recognition

One of the most notable proposals under discussion is the introduction of additional CRS consideration for high-wage occupations.

If implemented, applicants working in well-paid occupations that make significant contributions to the Canadian economy may benefit from additional advantages during the selection process.

This would place greater emphasis on both occupation and economic contribution than under the current system.

4. Some Existing CRS Bonus Points May Change

At the same time, several existing CRS bonus point categories may be revised or removed, including additional points for certain Canadian educational credentials, French-language proficiency, and having a sibling in Canada.

These proposals have not yet been finalized, and applicants should continue to monitor future announcements from IRCC.

Who May Benefit Most?

Based on the policy direction released so far, the following groups may be better positioned under the future system:

  • Applicants with Canadian work experience;
  • Professionals in high-demand sectors such as healthcare, education, and skilled trades;
  • Senior managers and other high-wage professionals;
  • Candidates whose occupations align with future category-based selection priorities.

Applicants whose competitiveness relies primarily on current CRS bonus points should closely monitor upcoming policy changes and consider planning their applications earlier where possible.

Looking Ahead

Canada is not reducing its commitment to economic immigration. Rather, it is redefining the type of talent it wishes to attract.

In the coming years, occupation, Canadian work experience, and alignment with labour market priorities may become even more important than simply achieving a high CRS score.

For prospective immigrants, understanding these trends early and planning accordingly may provide a significant advantage as Canada’s immigration system continues to evolve.

This article is based on current policy announcements, publicly available information, and recent Express Entry trends. Final policy details and implementation timelines remain subject to official announcements by IRCC.
